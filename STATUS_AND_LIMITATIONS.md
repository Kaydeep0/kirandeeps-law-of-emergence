# PART 4: LIMITATIONS & OPEN QUESTIONS

## 4.1 Acknowledged Limitations

### Selection Bias in Backtests
The 6 historical cases were chosen because data was available. A systematic survey of technology transitions with E calculations would strengthen the claim.

### Curve-Fitting vs. Derivation
The cosmological formulas (π^(3/2), e^(3/4)) were initially found by fitting, then interpreted. The interpretations (3 dimensions / 2 matter types, etc.) are post-hoc but consistent.

### Fine Structure Constant Gap
α = π^(-μ/8) gives 94% accuracy. The 6% error suggests either:
- A missing correction term
- A different relationship
- Fundamental limitation of the framework

### Measurement of E
Real-world E calculations require subjective choices:
- What counts as "certainty gained"?
- How to convert costs to energy?
- What time window to use?

The framework provides guidance but not unique answers.

## 4.2 Open Questions

### Q1: Why these specific formulas?

Why is dark matter π^(3/2) and not π^1.4 or π^1.6?

**Proposed answer:** 3/2 = dimensions/matter_types is the simplest ratio involving the fundamental count (3 dimensions) and the observed count (2 matter types).

**Open:** Is there a deeper derivation from first principles?

### Q2: Does higher E always win?

**Current answer:** Yes, eventually, with timing determined by friction.

**Open:** Are there cases where lower E wins permanently (not just temporarily)?

### Q3: How to measure consciousness?

**Proposed:** E threshold at μ ≈ 30

**Open:** What instrument measures this? What's the operational definition of "conscious"?

### Q4: What are the correction terms?

The ℏ derivation has corrections: 1/3, π/(54μ²), 55/(6072μ³)

**Open:** Where do these specific numbers come from? Is there a pattern?

### Q5: What's the next emergence level?

**Proposed:** Level 6 = awareness of the law itself

**Open:** What comes after? Can we predict Level 7?
